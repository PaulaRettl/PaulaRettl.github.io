Paula Rettl

Ph.D. candidate

Bocconi University

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Social and Political Sciences at Bocconi University. My research focuses on the comparative political economy of political behavior in Brazil and Europe.

My dissertation book project analyzes the political consequences of economic volatility. More specifically, I focus on behavioral responses to economic volatility in the mass public and political elites. The project employs a combination of survey and administrative data to unveil behavioral responses to economic shocks, taking into account local and institutional contexts.

Since June 2020, I am also a Ph.D. associate at the ERC-funded LOSS project (PI: Catherine De Vries), which analyzes how economic hardship fosters negative attitudes towards minority groups.

Prior to starting my Ph.D., I worked at the OECD, United Nations University and European Policy Center. I also earned a MA in European Affairs with a concentration on Politics and Public Policy from Sciences Po Paris and a BA in Geography from the State University of Campinas (Unicamp).
